Picadillo

Yield: 8 Servings

Time: 30 Minutes

Description

Picadillo… pronounced pee-kah-dee-yaw [pikaˈðiʎo], looks and sounds so exotic and, yes, so Spanish! This classic Latin ground beef dish comes in many forms, but this Puerto Rican version is one of my favorites. This picadillo is so versatile, great in tacos, burritos, empanadas or alongside rice and plantains.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs ground beef
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1/2 green bell pepper
  • 1/2 red bell pepper
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper
  • 3 tbsp. garlic
  • 4 tbsp. sofrito
  • 1 packet Sazon seasoning
  • 2 tsp. oregano
  • 2 tsp. cumin
  • 14 oz tomato sauce
  • 1/3 cup pimento stuffed olives
  • 2 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 tbsp. cilantro

Method

  1. Season ground beef with adobo.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions, bell peppers and garlic,sauté until onion is translucent.
  3. Add sofrito and sauté until fragrant and a little oil starts separating from your veggies.
  4. Add ground beef, crumble, and stir until browned, about 7-10 minutes.
  5. Add Sazon seasoning, oregano, cumin, and tomato sauce. Continue stirring.
  6. Add olives, cilantro and a little water. Cover and simmer for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until meat is nicely cooked and oil starts separating on top of meat.
  7. Serve over white rice. Enjoy!
